Station Salvation

Overview

Station Salvation is a third-person sci-fi shooter set amidst an assault on an expansive space station. The goal of the game is to traverse as far as as you can while eliminating as many invading aliens as possible before reaching the end. This video game was created for Game Design 2 course during my second semester at CSU East Bay.

Plot

An armed survivor is tasked with clearing as much space of hostiles as they can, while making their way to the extraction point.

Setting

The game takes place on a space station deep in space, far into the future. A foreign group of aliens have boarded the station, eliminating any humans on sight.

Gameplay Loop

  • Utilize primary weapon to combat alien opponents

Restraints

Since this was a school project, and taking into account the limitaions of the hardware we were using, as well as the limited amount of time, we were not able to accomplish all the goals we have set for ourselves and had to scrap a majority of our combined concepts.

My Role

During this project, I worked with four other students and acted as the project lead as well as the head developer/blueprint manager. The task that was entrusted to me was to lead the group in a direction that would allow for optimal productivity, as well as provide code that would allow the basic mechanics of the game to function, i.e., combat system, health system, and enemy encounters.

Process

For the initial process of Station Salvation I started to sketch a basic layout of the playable areas, giving both the map/level designer and the asset/texture designer of the group a base understanding of what we are trying to accomplish.

Once the tasks had been distributed and everyone understood their assignments, I then started to work on coding/blueprinting the combat mechanics, health system, and movement for both the playerable character and enemies.

After I had finished my tasks and my classmates had completed theirs, we then combined all our progress into a single unreal project and began to test for any errors.
